Chiles en Nogada


...

Chiles en Nogada is a traditional Mexican dish that is especially popular during the country's independence celebrations due to its patriotic colors. It is a festive and flavorful dish that originated in the state of Puebla. Chiles en Nogada is typically prepared with poblano peppers, which are large, mild chili peppers.


The dish is characterized by three main components:

  • Poblano Peppers: dish starts with poblano peppers that are roasted, peeled, and then stuffed with a mixture of ground meat, fruits, nuts, and spices. The ground meat is often a combination of beef and pork.
  • Nogada Sauce:The stuffed peppers are covered with a creamy walnut sauce called "nogada." The sauce is made with walnuts, cream, and a touch of sherry or white wine. The nogada sauce is rich and has a slightly sweet flavor.
  • Toppings: The dish is traditionally garnished with pomegranate seeds and chopped fresh parsley. The pomegranate seeds contribute a sweet and tart flavor while also adding vibrant red and white colors to the dish, symbolizing the colors of the Mexican flag.
